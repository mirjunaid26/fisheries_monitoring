Abstract. Overfishing threats require scalable monitoring solutions. We present SimpleFishNet, a custom lightweight Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) designed for simultaneous species classification and object detection. By leveraging a shared backbone and Multi-Task Learning (MTL), our model achieves efficient performance on the "The Nature Conservancy Fisheries Monitoring" dataset. Trained on community-verified bounding box annotations, SimpleFishNet demonstrates that compact, custom architectures can effectively monitor fisheries environments without the computational overhead of massive pre-trained models.

Dataset & Challenges

The dataset comprises imagery from commercial fishing vessels, featuring 8 distinct classes including Albacore (ALB), Bigeye Tuna (BET), and Sharks. The data reflects the chaotic nature of wild environments:

  • Class Imbalance: Follows Zipf's law, with rare protected species (Sharks) vastly outnumbered by target Tuna species.
  • Environmental Noise: Includes harsh glare, water droplets, and pitch-black night footage.
  • Occlusion: Fish are frequently overlapped or obscured by gear.

Methodology

Methodology

1. Architecture: SimpleFishNet

We implemented a custom 5-layer CNN from scratch. The network features a Single Shared Backbone composed of 4 convolutional blocks (Conv2D → ReLU → MaxPool) that extract hierarchical features. This backbone feeds into two parallel heads:

  • Classification Head: Supports 8-class species identification (Softmax).
  • Regression Head: Predicts bounding boxes coordinates (Sigmoid).

2. Loss Function

To handle the dual objective, we employed a weighted Multi-Task Loss: $$L_{total} = L_{cls} + \lambda L_{box}$$ where $L_{cls}$ is Cross-Entropy Loss and $L_{box}$ is Mean Squared Error (MSE) for localization.

3. Data & Training

The model was trained on the Nature Conservancy dataset using Official Community Bounding Boxes sourced from GitHub. We avoided complex pre-training to verify the architecture's innate ability to learn features from raw data.

Results

The proposed holistic pipeline delivers superior robustness compared to baseline methods. The integration of Zero-DCE++ extended effective monitoring hours to 24/7.
